Process & Technique

Each piece in this collection emerges from an analog photographic process that prioritizes patience and contemplation over immediate results. The work engages with the fundamental chemistry of photography, allowing the medium itself to participate in the creation of meaning.

Using traditional film and printing techniques, these images are developed through a dialogue between artist, subject, and material. The imperfections and variations inherent in analog processes become integral to the work's aesthetic and conceptual framework.

The resulting prints carry the physical trace of their making – a quality that connects them to the handmade traditions of photography while speaking to contemporary concerns about time, place, and presence in our increasingly digital world.
